Start Planning Your career From Here

Buid up your career Roadmap

Begin by exploring fields such as Data, UX, Computer Science, Library, and Archives to gain a broad understanding of the roles, responsibilities, and skills each area requires. Researching career paths and emerging trends can help identify which direction aligns best with individual interests and strengths. Gaining hands-on experience through internships, volunteer work, or personal projects is essential for building practical skills and confidence. Attending career events, networking with professionals, and setting clear short- and long-term goals will help maintain focus and momentum throughout the career planning journey.

Working Internationally

International working experience provides a wealth of abilities that extends far beyond technical skills. International work exposes professionals to different workplace cultures, communication styles, and problem-solving approaches, fostering adaptability and cultural intelligence.

It often involves navigating challenges such as language barriers, unfamiliar regulations, or new team dynamics, which helps build resilience and a global mindset. These experiences not only enrich professional development but also provide a broader outlook and valuable perspectives that can enhance future roles in both local and international settings.

Emphasizing your international working experience offer you distinctive shining points in resume and more oppertunities to gain ideal jobs. To learn how to add international career experience, CDO offers information on Highlighting Your International Experiences to Employers (presentation).
